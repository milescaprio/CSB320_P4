Four sets of parameters are selected.

1 is the default parameters passed, for control.

2 has a higher learning rate and also less epsilon decay (higher value). Maybe this allows for a more "creative" model, that while potentially varies more in outcome, may discover more creative approaches. However, it will train for longer.

3 has a higher learning rate but starts with a lower epsilon value. This model might be more "impatient" and might converge more efficiently, but also may have a greater chance of failure.

4 has a higher epsilon min and greater decay (lower value), allowing it to be "creative" more evenly throughout. Additionally, it has a lower gamma value, pressuring it to get to the rewards quickly.
